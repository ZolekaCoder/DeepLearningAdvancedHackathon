## 1. Executive Summary

- **Task.** Answer sexual-&-reproductive-health questions across **8 language-country subsets** (English + Akan, Amharic, Luganda, Swahili) from Ghana, Uganda, Kenya, Ethiopia. Train has **29,815** Q/A rows; the submission is scored on **three** targets — ROUGE-L F1, ROUGE-1 F1 and an **LLM judge** (`TargetRLF1`, `TargetR1F1`, `TargetLLM`).
- **Answers are largely canonical, not individually written.** **39%** of answers are exact duplicates and only 18,078 answers are unique; ~29% are near-duplicates. Many distinct questions map to one answer.
- **Retrieval works — and dense beats hybrid.** With *zero training*, copying the nearest Train answer via **dense e5** scores ROUGE-L **0.442**, ROUGE-1 **0.508**, exact **0.263**, topic-match **0.905** on Val — beating BM25 and a naive RRF hybrid.
- **But quality is wildly uneven by subset.** English-Kenya/Uganda and Swahili/Luganda are nearly solved by copy (ROUGE-L 0.47–0.66); Amharic, Akan and English-Ghana are not (0.17–0.21).
- **The gap is partly a metric artifact.** For Amharic/Akan the retriever finds the right, on-topic answer (topic-match 0.97–0.99) yet word-ROUGE stays low because these languages are morphologically rich — word overlap under-scores correct answers. The LLM judge will be fairer there.
- **No distribution shift and no cross-lingual parallelism.** Val and Test are ≈identical to Train (nearest-Train cosine ~0.96); the language subsets are **not** translations of each other (0% shared IDs).
- **Recommendation (headline).** Ship a **dense-retrieval-first, confidence-routed hybrid**: copy/rerank when retrieval is confident; generate with a multilingual LLM (retrieved answer as context) when it is not — especially for Amharic/Akan/Eng_Ghana. Evaluate and tune **per subset**.

## 3. Major Insights

**1. Canonical answer bank.** 39% exact-duplicate answers, 18,078 unique; one answer reused up to 68×. Answers are assembled from reusable per-topic blocks (e.g. the STI trivia and closings in `04_answer_repeated_sentences`).

**2. Eng_Eth topic-tag prefix.** 2,215 Eng_Eth answers (only) begin 'This is a question about, <TOPIC>.' — an exploitable style rule AND near-free gold topic labels.

**3. Dense > hybrid > BM25.** Dense e5 retrieval is the strongest zero-training signal; naive RRF hybrid and BM25 are weaker (`10_retrieval_headline`).

**4. Per-subset retrieval variance.** ROUGE-L: Eng_Ken 0.66 / Swa_Ken 0.62 / Eng_Uga 0.61 / Lug_Uga 0.47 (strong) vs Eng_Gha 0.21 / Akan 0.20 / Amharic 0.17 (weak).

**5. ROUGE vs morphology.** Amharic/Akan: high topic-match (0.97–0.99) but low ROUGE — word-overlap metrics under-score morphologically rich correct answers (`13_complexity_answers`, `07_oov_train_val_questions`: Amharic 16% token-OOV).

**6. Automatic topics.** BERTopic discovered 67 data-driven health topics (HIV/AIDS, HPV, PrEP/PEP, mother-child/breastfeeding, puberty, peer-pressure, digital literacy…); mean cluster purity vs Eng_Eth gold ≈0.59 (`08_topic_*`).

**7. No translation / parallelism.** 0% of IDs shared across subsets; non-English↔English cosine 0.85–0.87 vs 0.94–0.98 English–English (`06_*`). Independently authored per language.

**8. No distribution shift.** Val & Test ≈ Train (nearest-Train cosine ~0.96, p10 0.93); language mix matches within ±5pp (`14_*`, `15_*`). Val is a trustworthy proxy for Test.

**9. Style: minimal disclaimers.** Safety disclaimers appear <1% of the time; bullets <2%. A generator must NOT add boilerplate or it loses ROUGE (`12_*`).

## 4. Risks

1. **Language imbalance.** Eng_Uga 25.6% vs Amh_Eth 6.2% (4.1× ratio). Pooled training over-serves English; per-subset metrics count equally.
2. **Low-resource quality.** Amharic/Akan/Eng_Ghana retrieval is weak; these drag the mean and are hardest to generate for.
3. **Metric mismatch.** Word-ROUGE penalises correct morphologically-rich answers; optimising ROUGE alone can hurt the LLM-judge score and vice-versa.
4. **Ge'ez script.** Amharic uses a different script; tokenizer/embedding coverage must be verified or retrieval & generation silently degrade.
5. **Answer ambiguity ceiling.** ~4% of questions have >1 valid gold answer — a hard cap on exact/ROUGE for any system.
6. **Retrieval failure tail.** A minority of low-similarity questions (e.g. Eng_Gha, novel Test items) cannot be answered by copy and need generation.
7. **Long answers.** Akan/Eng_Uga answers reach ~450+ words; truncation or length mismatch costs ROUGE and coherence.
8. **Over-cleaning.** Stripping the Eng_Eth prefix or adding disclaimers would diverge from gold; post-processing must be subset-aware.

## 5. Recommended Architecture (evidence-based)

A **confidence-routed hybrid of dense retrieval + multilingual generation**:

1. **Index** the Train corpus (unique answers + their questions) with `multilingual-e5-base` question embeddings. *Evidence:* dense retrieval is the strongest zero-training baseline (§2.4) and the answer bank is canonical (§3.1).
2. **Retrieve** top-k Train questions per query (dense; BM25 only as a tuned add-on / rare-term fallback — naive RRF hurt, §3.3).
3. **Rerank** top-k with a cross-encoder or answer-side similarity; the Q→A coupling gap (0.87 vs 0.77 shuffled) confirms question-based retrieval is meaningful (`11_qa_coupling`).
4. **Route by confidence** (nearest-Train cosine, tuned on Val since Val≈Test, §3.8): **high** → return/lightly-edit the retrieved canonical answer (cheap, high ROUGE for Ken/Uga/Swa/Lug); **low** → **generate** with a multilingual instruction-tuned LLM using the retrieved answer(s) as context (RAG), for Amharic/Akan/Eng_Ghana and novel questions.
5. **Post-process per subset**: match target length/style, prepend the Eng_Eth topic prefix, and do NOT add unsolicited disclaimers (§3.9, `12_*`).
6. **Evaluate per subset** on both ROUGE and a semantic/LLM-judge proxy; do not optimise a single global ROUGE (§3.5).

**Model-choice guidance:**

- *Retrieval-only:* strong, cheap baseline; near-solves Kenya/Uganda subsets but leaves Amharic/Akan/Eng_Ghana far behind → **insufficient alone**.
- *Retrieval + generation (RAG):* recommended — grounds the LLM in canonical answers, closes the low-resource gap, and controls style.
- *Fine-tuning / LoRA:* likely helps for style-matching and low-resource languages; LoRA is a cost-effective way to adapt a multilingual decoder without full fine-tuning. Use subset-balanced sampling (§4.1).
- *Multilingual seq2seq (e.g. mT5/NLLB-style):* reasonable for short-answer subsets (Amharic/Eng_Eth) but weaker for long free-form answers; **decoder-only instruction-tuned multilingual LLMs** are the more flexible default.
- *Hybrid > pure generator:* yes — retrieval provides the canonical content the metric rewards; generation provides coverage and fluency where copy fails.

## 6. Modelling Decisions (digest — full list in `MODELLING_DECISIONS.md`)

### High priority

- **language-imbalance** — Per-subset metrics; subset-balanced sampling/loss weighting; non-English-dominated retrieval.
- **amharic-script** — Verify Ge'ez coverage; evaluate Amharic separately; script-aware normalization.
- **answer-duplication-canonical** — Retrieval-first baseline; build corpus from unique answers; treat partly as answer selection.
- **eng-eth-topic-prefix** — Parse to a topic column; post-process Eng_Eth predictions to prepend the prefix; don't leak elsewhere.
- **retrieval-feasibility** — Adopt dense retrieval baseline; use retrieved answer as RAG context; rerank top-k.
- **dense-beats-hybrid** — Default to dense; add BM25 only via TUNED fusion / rare-term fallback; validate any hybrid beats dense.
- **retrieval-quality-by-subset** — Route by subset+confidence: copy where dense-sim high; generate for Amharic/Akan/Eng_Gha.
- **cross-lingual-parallelism** — No pivot-through-English assumption; retrieve within-language; treat cross-lingual reuse as topical.
- **no-distribution-shift** — Tune routing/rerank thresholds on Val and trust for Test; extra attention to Eng_Gha.
- **rouge-morphology-gap** — Report per-subset ROUGE; char/stemmed ROUGE diagnostics; optimise these subsets toward the LLM judge.

### Medium priority

- **question-templating** — Use dense/semantic retrieval esp. for African languages; intent clustering for few-shot exemplars.
- **answer-ambiguity-ceiling** — Prefer semantic eval; pick the most frequent/representative answer among variants.
- **topic-inventory** — Store topic ids as metadata; topic filter in retrieval; report metrics per topic.
- **qa-coupling** — Rely on question retrieval; optionally add answer-space reranking.
- **answer-style-and-disclaimers** — Match per-subset length & plain prose; suppress unsolicited disclaimers.
- **oov-subword-tokenization** — Use subword/byte tokenizers and dense retrieval for these languages.
